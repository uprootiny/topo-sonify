Loops add modulation

← Prev Next →
0.15

Six points form a hexagonal ring. Increase the radius slowly.

At the right radius, all neighbors connect but the ring isn't filled. You've created a 1-cycle — a closed loop of edges that doesn't bound any triangle.

This loop is β₁ = 1. Each independent loop adds a low-frequency oscillator (LFO) that modulates the sound. A cycle is periodic — and periodicity is modulation.

Keep increasing radius. When triangles fill in, the loop dies — it becomes the boundary of a surface. β₁ drops to 0. The modulation stops.

The birth and death of this loop is a persistence pair.
