Pick a space. Hit Clap to hear its reverb.

The Euler characteristic χ measures curvature. By the Gauss-Bonnet theorem: ∫K dA = 2πχ. Total curvature is fixed by topology.

Positive χ (sphere): geodesics converge. Sound focuses. Short, bright reverb — like a dome.

Zero χ (torus): flat on average. Neutral reverb — like a room. Negative χ (high genus): geodesics diverge. Sound scatters. Long, dark, diffuse reverb — like a cave.

This is not a metaphor. The wave equation on a curved manifold has decay rate governed by curvature. Negative curvature = slower decay.
